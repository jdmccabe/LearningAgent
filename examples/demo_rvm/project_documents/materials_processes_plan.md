# Materials and Processes Control Plan

Document: ATGSD-MP-PLAN-012
Revision: D

Materials exposed to oxygen service, ignition sources, or enclosed operator work areas are screened for flammability, odor, offgassing, and compatibility using project tailoring derived from NASA-STD-6001B.

Elastomeric seals in the pressure manifold use project-approved EPDM compound EPDM-445-70. Incoming inspection verifies cure date, dimensional class, batch traceability, and compression-set certificate against MSFC-SPEC-445 project procurement notes.

Exterior carbon steel frame members are hot-dip galvanized after fabrication. Coating thickness is inspected at five locations per member and must be greater than or equal to 85 micrometers average thickness with no bare areas.

Formed sheet panels use G90 zinc-coated ASTM A653 steel with material certifications retained in the manufacturing record.

Cadmium plating is prohibited for oxygen-system and crew-accessible hardware. Waiver ATGSD-WVR-003 permits legacy cadmium plating only on one sealed commercial relay inside the electrical cabinet; the relay is outside oxygen service and has no routine operator contact.

Heat-treated aluminum brackets and steel fasteners require furnace run records, load thermocouple identification, quench timing, and material certification. Shot peening and passivation are accepted only when specified on released drawings.

Packaging for critical parts uses desiccant bags, humidity indicator cards, protective caps, FOD covers, and shock-watch labels during transport.
