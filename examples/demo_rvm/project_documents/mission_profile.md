# Mission Profile and Boundary Description

Program: Artemis Training Ground Support Demonstrator
Configuration: ATGSD-GSE-1, Revision B
Date: 2026-04-30

The project is a non-flight, ground support equipment demonstrator used for launch-team training and procedure validation. The article never flies, carries no crew, and does not include explosive, pyrotechnic, or energetic devices.

The demonstrator interfaces with a flight-hardware simulator through electrical umbilical connectors, a nitrogen purge panel, and a low-pressure oxygen service simulator used for cleanliness and compatibility training. The system includes no wireless transmitters, no radio telemetry, and no battery-powered remote-control functions.

The operating envelope includes indoor checkout, transport to a concrete pad, crane lift using a certified spreader beam, nitrogen proof and leak checks, oxygen-compatible component handling, and supervised simulated fueling. Maximum design pressure is 450 psig for process piping and 600 psig for a removable certified accumulator.

The project uses a tailored mission assurance basis. Safety-critical hazards and pressure boundaries are treated as DAL B. Electrical controls, wiring workmanship, and GSE programmable logic are treated as DAL C. Passive corrosion-protection and packaging requirements are treated as DAL D.

Applicable lifecycle objectives are tracked in ATGSD-RVM-PLAN-001 and use project-specific objective identifiers such as RS-1, STR-1, ELEC-1, WIRE-1, PRES-1, GSE-1, MAT-1, and PACK-1.
